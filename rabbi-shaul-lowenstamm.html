Rav Shaul Löwenstamm (Amsterdammer), the Binyan Ariel

1717- 1790

Biography

Rav Shaul was born in Rzezów (Poland). He was the son of Rav Arye Leib Löwenstamm who was the son in law of the Chacham Tsvi. He was also the great grandson of the rebbe Reb Heshel.

He became Rav in Lakacz and Dubno. In 1755 his father, the av beis din of Amsterdam was nifter and now Rav Shaul became Av beis din in Amsterdam until his petire in 1790 on 7 tamuz 5650. He was nifter on shabbes around minche time and buried the next day. It is told that at the moment the mitah came outside it started pouring in Amsterdam and the moment the mitah was outside Amsterdam, the skies cleared up.

He is known as the author of the sefer on chumash Binyan Ariel (Amsterdam, 1778), which title he chose as a pun on “Ben Arye”. He also wrote the perush on chumash Hechatser Hachadasha (Amsterdam1768). In 1828 he published his Halacha Lema’ase Rav, containing a psak haloche on the kashrus of Dutch cheese. Rav Shaul devoted much attention to the beis hamidrash founded by his father.

In the year 1778 the Chida came to Amsterdam and visited Rav Shaul. The Chida wrote in his sefer Shem haGedolim that he felt it was like receiving the countenance of the shechina.

Major Works

Binyan Ariel (Amsterdam, 1778) – A commentary on the Chumash. The title is a pun on “Ben Arye”.

Hechatser Hachadasha (Amsterdam1768) – A commentary on the Chumash.

Halacha Lema’ase Rav (1828) – Contains a psak haloche on the kashrus of Dutch cheese.

Rav Shaul also devoted much attention to the beis hamidrash founded by his father.

Visiting the Kever

The kever of the Binyan Ariel is in Muiderberg. The address is: Googweg 6, 1399 MUIDERBERG

It takes 1 hour and 25 mins. by train from Amsterdam and 20 mins.by car.
