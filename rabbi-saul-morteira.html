Rav Shaul Levi Morteira

1596-1660

Biography

Rav Shaul Levi Morteira was born in Venice into a family of Spanish refugees. In Venice he learned at the yeshive and at a young age he moved to Paris together with the physician Elijah Montalto, physician of the French queen, for whom he worked as a secretary. After the petire of the physician he took care of the kevure which had to be done in Amsterdam where a Jewish community had been openly established and he could have a Jewish burial in the Beth Haim beis hakvores in Ouderkerk, near Amsterdam.

In Amsterdam he was asked by the Portuguese Beth Jaakob kehila to become their Chacham. He was appointed in 1618 at the young age of twenty-two. He continued learning Torah by Chacham Yitzchak Uziel who was the Rav of the second Portuguese kehila. Most of the members of his kehila were Marranos who had been living as “New Christians” under the Inquisition for over a hundred years, keeping what they knew of Jewish practices, but without much Jewish education.

Rav Morteira had a fomidable challenge before him, to both bring them to want to observe full Judaism, and also teach them what this means. His long droshes on shabbes and yom tov were designed to draw the adults of the kehilla closer to Judaism, both in knowledge and in practice. He used his extensive knowledge and brilliant genius to explain Judaism, refute Christian concepts and interpretations, and influence his listeners to make the effort and raise their standards of observance.

For the children of the kehila he taught Talmud in the local school for long hours and would also teach them Parashas Hashavua. In the year 1643 he established the Yeshiva Keter Torah for those who wanted to learn to become rabonim. Among its graduates were the well-known Rav Moshe Zacuto and Rav Avraham Pimentel, author of Minchas Cohen on halacha.

Simultaneously Rav Shaul served as Rav of his kehila, principal of the school, and Rosh Yeshiva and Av Bes Din (Rav Menashe ben Yisroel served as a dayan in his Beis Din). His mark on the community was successful and eternal. His tremendous knowledge and untiring efforts truly transformed hundreds of families of ignorant refugees from Spain and Portugal into a strong and knowledgeable Jewish Orthodox community, while training the most gifted of his talmidim to become first-rate rabonim capable of leading the future generations in his footsteps.

Rav Morteira and Rav Yitzchak Aboab da Fonseca were members of the Ma’amad which on 27 July pronounced the decree of cherem against Spinoza (Rav Menashe ben Yisroel was at that time in England).

Rav Shaul Morteira was nifter in 1660 and buried in de bes hakvores of Ouderkerk.

Major Works

The droshes of Rav Morteira were printed in the year 1645 in Amsterdam by his talmidim. The sefer Givat Shaul contains fifty droshes (selected from 500 droshes) that always start with a posuk from the parshe of the week followed by a statement from the Talmud and then the Rav ingeniously and eloquently addresses a topic of interest at lenght. He often gives chizuk to the Marranos and praises them while also sometimes expressing his sadness about the Conversos who chose a life of riches in Spain and Portugal instead of life in an open Jewish world.

He also wrote in Spanish Tractado de la Verdad de la Ley, translated in Hebrew by Yitzchak Gomez de Gosa under the title Torat Moshe, apologetics of Judaism and attacks against Christianity.
