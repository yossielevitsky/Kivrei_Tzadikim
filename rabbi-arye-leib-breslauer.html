Rav (Levi) Arye Leib Heiman Breslauer (The Pne Arye)

1741-1809

Biography

Rav Arye Leib was born in Breslau. He was a descendant of the Remo (Rav Moshe Isserlis) and a great-grandson of the Chacham Tsvi. He became Rav in Berlin and then in Emden. In 1781 he came to Rotterdam to lead the kehilla.

He had strong opinions in halachic matters, a famous example being his stand against jewish married women covering their hair with a sheitel instead of a scarf.

In 1811 it became obligatory in the Netherlands to choose a surname. The widow of the Pne Arye then chose the name Löwenstamm (Löwe meaning lion in german, after her husbands first name Arye)

Rav Arye was loved by jews and non-jews alike.

He was nifter suddenly on Friday night the 21th of april 1809, mourned by the members of his kehille and also by many non-jews. His almone Reshka was nifteres on october 24th 1825. Both are buried in the beis hakvores Oostzeedijk in Rotterdam.

Major Works

He wrote a well-known responsa work dealing with the four sections of the Shulchan Oruch, Pne Arye, printed by the famous printing and publishing house Proops in Amsterdam. This contributed greatly to his stature as one of the greatest halachic authorities of his time.

Visiting the Kever

The beis hakvores is not easy to find, seen from the centre of Rotterdam it is situated on the right side of the Oostzeedijk behind the Infirmeriestraat.

The address is: Oostzeedijk 260-232      3063 BP Rotterdam
