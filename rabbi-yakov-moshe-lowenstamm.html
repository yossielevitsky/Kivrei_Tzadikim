Rav Yakov Moshe Löwenstamm

1747-1815

Biography

Rav Yakov Moshe was Rav of Wilheln (Germany) before he came to Amsterdam to assist his father (Rav Shaul Amsterdammer) in managing the affairs of the rabbinate in his old age in 1772. After his father’s petire the rabbinical seat of the Ashkenazi kehilla in Amsterdam was vacant for three years. In 1793 Rav Yakov Moshe began serving as Rav of Amsterdam until his petire.

From a (manuscript) hesped we learn the magnitude of the admiration and appreciation Rav Yakov Moshe had in his kehille; “...Our transgressions did this to us, causing the departure of our gaon and our glory, the crown of our heads, our good badge, he sanctified gem we had among us, our master, teacher and rabbi, the gaon, the holy luminary, whose name befit him, Yakov Moshe, son of the righteous and pious master….galah mesos libenu beyom simchasenu beyom purenu”; He was nifter on shushan purim.

Visiting the Kever
