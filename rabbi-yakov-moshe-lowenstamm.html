Rav Yakov Moshe Löwenstamm

1747-1815

Biography

Rav Yakov Moshe was Rav of Wilheln (Germany) before he came to Amsterdam in 1772 to assist his father ( Rav Shaul Amsterdammer) in his old age, in managing the affairs of the rabbinate. After his father’s petire the rabbinical seat of the Ashkenazi kehilla in Amsterdam was vacant for three years. In 1793 Rav Yakov Moshe began serving as Rav of Amsterdam until his petire.

From a (manuscript) hesped we learn the magnitude of the admiration and appreciation Rav Yakov Moshe had in his kehille; “...Our transgressions did this to us, causing the departure of our gaon and our glory, the crown of our heads, our good badge, he sanctified gem we had among us, our master, teacher and rabbi, the gaon, the holy luminary, whose name befit him, Yakov Moshe, son of the righteous and pious master….galah mesos libenu beyom simchasenu beyom purenu”; These last words refer to the day of shushan purim since he was nifter on shushan purim.

Visiting the Kever

It takes 1 hour and 25 mins. by train from Amsterdam and 20 mins.by car.
