Rav Arye Leib Löwenstamm

1690-1755

Biography

Rav Arye Leib was born in Krakow, Poland. His father was the Rav of Krakow and his grandfather was Reb Heshel of Krakow. In 1707 Rav Arye Leib married the oldest daughter of the Chacham Tsvi and continued his studies under his father in law.

His first known rabbinical position was in Dubno. Later he was elected rav of Dukla and then in Tarnopol Rzeszow, Glogow and Lviv. In 1740 he was appointed Rav of Amsterdam until his petire in 1755.

Rav Aryeh Leib became known for the active part he played in the controversy between Rav Yacov Emden and Yonathan Eybeschütz. He sided with the former who was his wife’s brother. He was the founder of the Beis Hamedrash Ets Chaim in Amsterdam which still exists today, although in a different location.
