Zvi Ashkenazi (Chacham Zvi)

1656, Moravia – 1718, Lviv

Biography

Zvi Ashkenazi, known as the Chacham Zvi, was a Talmudist and halakhic authority. He served as the rabbi of the Ashkenazi community in Amsterdam from 1710 to 1714.

He was known for his firm and independent character, often finding himself in controversy due to his unyielding stances on halakhic matters. He was a fierce opponent of the Sabbatean movement.

Major Works

She'elot u-Teshuvot Chacham Zvi – A collection of responsa that is highly regarded in halakhic literature for its brevity and clarity.

Historical Context

His tenure in Amsterdam coincided with a period of significant growth for the Ashkenazi community, which was beginning to rival the Sephardic community in numbers and influence.
