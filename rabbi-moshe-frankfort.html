Rav Moshe Frankfort

1672-1762

Biography

Rav Moshe was born in Amsterdam at a time when the ashkenazi kehille just started growing. The ashkenazim were not part anymore of the Portuguese kehille and they increased in numbers because of the arrival of refugees from Germany, Lithuania and Poland.

He succeeded his father after his petire as dayan and rosh chevre kadishe. The Chida writes in his Shem haGedolim that he wrote the sefer Zeh Yenachamenu (a peirush on Mechilta) and Be’er Hetev on Shulchan Oruch, Choshen Mishpat. The Chido is full of praise for his Be’er Hetev.

In 1721 he established a printing and publishing house in Amsterdam where he published his own seforim and other seforim in hebrew and in yiddish. He also edited a new edition of the Mikraos Gedolos. He translated his father’s Sefer Hachaim into Yiddish and published it in 1721.

He was nifter on 27 aw, 16th of august 1762 at the age of 90. He was buried in Muiderberg.

Major Works

Zeh Yenachamenu – A commentary on the Mechilta.

Be’er Hetev – A commentary on the Shulchan Oruch, Choshen Mishpat (highly praised by the Chida).

Mikraos Gedolos – Edited a new edition of this classic work.

Sefer Hachaim – Translated his father's work into Yiddish and published it in 1721.
