Rav Shaul haLevi Hager

1712-1785

Biography

Rav Shaul Hager (of Den Haag) was born in Dobromil (Poland) in a well to do family. His father was the chief Rabbi of the town, Rav Yitzchok haLevi.

Already in his youth he stood out for his lomdus and his yiras shomaim.

At a relatively young age he was appointed Rav in the prestigious kehille of Emden in Germany in 1740.

In 1746 the kehille of the Hague in the Netherlands asked him to take upon himself the mantle of leadership which he did until his petire in 1785. Under his leadership, the Hague became an important centre of the Jewish world with even a Jewish printing/publishing house.

His influence was great in and outside Europe and reached as far as the far east. When the Chida (Rav Chaim Yoseph Dovid Azulai) came to Amsterdam from Erets Yisroel, he also visited the Hague, paying an official visit to Rav Saul haLevi as soon as he arrived.

Even though he did not receive a big salary, he was a tremendous baal tsedoke. He was loved by everyone because of his kindness and friendliness. He was nosei b’ol with all yidden and their tsores. He used to fast every Monday and Thursday for fiftyfive years.

As a token of gratitude, the kehilla offered him a portrait painting, made by the jewish painter Abraham Polak. It’s one of very few portraits of gedolim of that generation that have been preserved.

His petira in 1785 caused deep mourning by everyone, he was brought to his resting place by members of his kehille, accompanied by non-jews and members of the governement.

Major Works

Binyan Shaul – A collection of sermons (drashot) and Torah novellae.

Visiting the Kever

From over the world, yidden come to daven by his kever. A metal case was donated by an american lady to prevent the yohrzeit candels from damaging the matseive.

It seems as if a giant hand protruding from a nearby oaktree keeps the matseive in place.

The matseive has been restored in 2017. His wife Dina is buried next to her husband. She was the daughter of Rav Arye Leib of Amsterdam who was the son in law of the Chacham Tzvi. She was the sister of Rav Shaul of Amsterdam (the Binyan Ariel).
