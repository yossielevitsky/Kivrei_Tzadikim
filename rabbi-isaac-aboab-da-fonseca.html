Rav Yitzchak Aboab da Fonseca

1605-1693

Biography

Rav Yitzchak Aboab was born into a family of conversos (Jews who had forcibly converted to Christianity) in Portugal. In 1612, when Yitzchak was seven, the family moved to Amsterdam. From that moment on, the family “reconverted” to Judaism. From 1603 it was legal in the Netherlands to practice Judaism in the open.

Rav Yitzchak studied together with Rav Menashe ben Yisroel under Chacham Yitzchak Uziel. At the age of eigtteen, Rav Yizchak was appointed Chacham for Beth Israel, one of three Sephardic kehilles in Amsterdam. In order to be distinguished from his cousin Yizchak ben Matityahu Aboab, he added his mother’s last name (da Fonseca) to his own.

He earned his living in the printing industry, along with other activities. The first Hebrew book was printed in Amsterdam in 1627 by Rav Menashe ben Yisroel, with Rav Aboab’s assistance who did the proofreading for this siddur.

Around 1641, Rav Yitzchak Aboab da Fonseca was invited by Moses Cohen Henriques to become Rav in Recife, Dutch Brazil. He was probably one of the first appointed Rabbonim of the Americas. Many Jews from Amsterdam followed him. When the Portuguese attacked the Dutch colony, the Jews fought alongside the Dutch army. After nine years of war, the Portuguese re-occupied Recife. Rav Aboab then returned to Amsterdam. Some members of his kehille immigrated to North America and were among the fouders of New Amsterdam.

Back in Amsterdam, Rav Yitzchak Aboab was appointed Rabbi for the Sephardic kehille. In 1656, he was one of several scholars who put a cherem on Baruch Spinoza.

When Rav Menashe ben Yisroel went to England (1656) Rav Aboab was in charge of the rabonus and after the petire of Chacham Morteira (1660) he became head of the Rabbinate and the Talmud Torah.

He was one of many fervent Sephardic supporters of Shabbetai Tzvi.

During his Rabanus, the Sefardi kehille flourished. The construction of the new Portuguese shul was prompted by a droshe delivered by him in 1671. The Esnoga was inaugurated less than four years later. The imposing building was lit up with candles, hakofes were made, Chacham Aboab carriying the first sefer tora. The songs that were sung were composed by himself and Shlomo d’Oliveyra. The name Aboab is to be seen in the words above the entrance to the shul; Va’ani berov chasdecha avo besecha. The letters aleph bes vav aleph bes in avo besecha spell Aboab.

Rav Yitzchak Aboab was nifter in Amsterdam and was succeeded by Rav Yakov Sasportas. His death was deeply felt by the members of the kehille such that, many years later, the name Aboab and the date of his death were still to be found on the engraved border of all kesubes of the Sephardi kehille.

Major Works

Rav Yizchak Aboab published several texts on Kabbala (one of them is Shaar Hashamaim).

He also wrote his memoirs about the difficult time during the fights in Recife; Zecher Asiti Leniflaot El.

When he was seventy-five years old, he wrote a perush on Chumash; Paraphrasis Comentada Sobre el Pentateucho (published in 1681).

Visiting the Kever

Rav Yitzchak's wife Ester was nifteres in 1669 and he bought a kever covered with a double stone in de beis hakvores of Ouderkerk. On the right side he had a text engraved and above it a wreath of leaves with the text: Eshes cha’il ateres baal. Underneath the text on the matseve a reclining hour-glass is engraved.

Rav Aboab remarried with Sara, whom he also survived. Her matseva is very near to the double matseva (a small blue matseva).

His matzeve is engraved with a pomegranate branch above the text and an olive branch hanging from a crown. Beneath the text lies an hourglass and between both sides stands a column with an open book and a little crown as well as on one page a burning candle. Beneath this a posuk from Yeshaya and out of a cloud appears an arm of which the hand holds a crowned F. The F refers to Fonseca. A two-armed lamp is placed above both slabs.

The text reminds us that Rav Aboab was nifter at the age of eighty eight and that he had led the kehilla of the Portuguese Jews for seventy years.
