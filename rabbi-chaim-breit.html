Rav Chaim ben Dovid Breit

ca. 1826-1884

Biography

Rav Chaim came from Dukla, nowadays in Poland not far from the Slovakian border. In Dukla the majority of the residents were Jews.

No one knows for sure why Rav Chaim Breit came to Amsterdam; he might have been fleeing the pogroms of 1881 in eastern Europe. Or maybe he wanted to print his sefer in Amsterdam. We know that his stay in Amsterdam was only meant to be temporary.

On erev rosh hashone 5744 Rav Chaim was nifter in the Nieuwe Kerkstraat 135 (the jewish hospital) in Amsterdam, in a part of town where a great number of Eastern European immigrants were living.

Major Works

Tsapachas Hashemen – A commentary on the sefer Kad Hakemach from Rabenu Bachye ben Osher (13th century). This commentary was published in Lemberg in 1880.

Visiting the Kever

He was buried on the second day rosh hashone in Zeeburg (a neighbourhood in the east of Amsterdam). The wellknown talmid chochom from Amsterdam Eliezer Liepman Philip Prins (1835-1915) took care of the placement of a matseive on his kever with his accomplishments written on it. In 2009 the matseive has been restored.

The beis hakvores is open every sunday between 10.00 and 16.00.

Only the kvorim that have been restored can be visited. One has to be aware of the fact that in this beis hakvores it is not possible to avoid walking on kvorim that are everywhere around.
